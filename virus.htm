Church Fenton - Covid-19 Information


Are you Impacted due to the Coronavirus? Although you may not need it now, a friend, family member, neighbour, or even you may require assistance. The following information aims to provide sources of help, advice and contact details to help you through the current difficulties.


Church Fenton Covid-19 Action Group

We are here to support and keep our community safe. We must stress the importance of staying at home, only leaving when absolutely necessary. All vulnerable groups and families with symptoms must self-isolate to protect our community. We continue to work with Tadcaster Community Action Group who are providing us with support. For up to date information and to request support, please contact us in the following ways:

Church Fenton Support - Tel: 0779 5071741 - Parish Clerk - see also the contact details on the Parish Council page

Wider Community Advice - Landline: 01937 326010

Would you like to volunteer? Please phone 0780 1965268

Facebook: Church Fenton Parish Council

If you need financial support at this time, we can access a variety of funding streams and grants to provide amongst other things, food vouchers. Don't be afraid, please get in touch if we can help you.

If there is anything that is not covered on this web page, or any particular help and support that you feel you require, please get in touch. Stay Safe and follow Government guidelines.


Red Card to Request Help

Firstly, and probably most important of all, we want to look after our neighbours. Your neighbours and our volunteers will be checking on everyone to make sure we are all safe. If everything is okay (you don't have to, but could put something green at your window), but if you need help, please put something RED against your window where people can see and someone will come to check on you. Anything red, (tea towel, t-shirt etc) will let us know, you need support (If you feel comfortable, also write your phone number in the window so that someone can call you). If we all do this, we can all help each other through these "unprecedented times".

If you see a RED signal but are unable to help, please call 0779 5071741. We can deliver a red card through your door if required. Please call the Clerk. Thank you and Keep Safe.


Practical Advice - For those feeling unwell

IN AN EMERGENCY - where the Defibrillator is required. It is positioned on the side of the Village Hall on Main Street, LS24 9RF. Should you need it in an emergency, the code is C147.

Prescription Delivery Service Support We would suggest you contact your Pharmacy or Medical Practice directly for your prescription. If you need support to collect medications, please phone the numbers above. If you have use APPS, the following 2 Apps are very useful:

Good SAM ALERTER - for non Covid-19 emergencies. Developed by the London Ambulance Service and London Hospital, This is a global app to alert a Medic who may be located near you.

Covid-19 join Zoe.com - COVID-19 Symptom Tracker App Set up by King's College, Guys and St Thomas's Hospital. Lets anyone healthy or with symptoms share valuable information. You can report your health status daily.

Other sources of advice you may find useful are:

NHS website https://www.nhs.uk/conditions/coronavirus-covid-19/

Government website https://www.gov.uk/coronavirus


Volunteers Needed

The Church Fenton Covid-19 Action Group are looking for volunteers. If you think you can help support our local community in this hour of need, please contact 07801 965268.

We are particularly looking for a key contact on each street to check for red posters in the window of "those needing help", and deliveries in your area (either hot meals, prescriptions or food).

Do you have a medical background? If a neighbour or someone in the Parish is unwell, would you be happy to give advice or support medically? If you are prepared to do this, let us know and we may put you in contact with someone needing advice and support.

Activities

The following are a number of ideas/activities to help keep you and your children entertained and active over this unusual period.

Tales of Church Fenton

We hope that you are all coping with the government lockdown and are supporting your neighbours. We need your help. We would like you to take this time out of our normal daily lives to reflect on where you live and our surroundings. It's good to talk, we want everyone to talk to one another, either through video calling or on your daily exercise. We are thinking about organising a "Tales of Church Fenton" publication.

Whether you have lived in the village all your life, have seen the changes to our landscape or you are new to the area, we would like to hear from you. You can interview your neighbour over the fence, if you know of someone who lives alone, you can visit them, talk to them through a window or over the garden fence (keeping your social distance at all times). If you live alone or would like to write your own tales of life in the village, please do so.

We haven't yet decided what form this publication will take, but the reality is, this all depends on you. We want this to be all about our residents. We would particularly like to hear from those of you who have lived here a long time, reminisce about the days of Air Displays and the Junction, the Pathfinders group or the Youth Club in the Old School, whatever your story, we would like to hear from you. It would be great to hear from anyone from 4yrs old to 104 years old!

Once you have written your tales, please drop it off or send it over.

Drop off point: Village Hall postbox or email: clerk.churchfentonpc@gmail.com
What we need: A story of your life in the village
The end result: We will compile all your anecdotes
